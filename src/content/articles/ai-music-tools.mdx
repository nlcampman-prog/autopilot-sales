# 7 Best AI Music Tools for Producers: Text-to-MIDI, Chords, and Vocals

There has always been a tension between technology and traditional music production. From the introduction of the synthesizer to the rise of Auto-Tune, new tools have often been met with skepticism before becoming industry standards.

In 2024, we are witnessing the next great shift: **Generative AI in the studio.**

But unlike the headlines might suggest, AI isn't here to replace the producer. Instead, it’s acting as the ultimate "studio assistant"—a collaborator that can help you break through writer’s block, generate complex MIDI sequences in seconds, or separate a vocal from a noisy recording.

Whether you're a bedroom producer or a seasoned pro, these are the 7 best AI music tools you should be using right now.

---

## The Three Pillars of AI Music Tech

To understand where to invest your time, it’s helpful to categorize AI music tools into three areas:

1.  **Generative (Full Songs):** Tools that create a complete audio file from a text prompt. Great for inspiration or reference tracks.
2.  **Assistive (MIDI & Chords):** Tools that generate the building blocks of a song, which you then edit and arrange in your DAW (Digital Audio Workstation).
3.  **Utility (Stem Separation & Mastering):** Tools that handle the technical "heavy lifting" of audio engineering.

## 7 Best AI Music Tools for 2024

### 1. Suno AI & Udio (Best for Full Song Inspiration)
Suno and Udio are the "Midjourney of Music." You type in a prompt like "1970s psychedelic rock song about a lonely astronaut," and they generate a full, high-fidelity track with lyrics and vocals.

*   **Key Features:** High-quality vocal synthesis, incredible genre awareness, and the ability to "extend" a clip into a full 4-minute song.
*   **Best For:** Brainstorming melodies, creating quick reference tracks, or overcoming the "blank project" syndrome.
*   **Affiliate Potential:** Low (Subscription-based with limited referral programs).

### 2. ChordGen (Best for Text-to-Chords)
Struggling to find the right progression for your track? ChordGen allows you to describe the "vibe" you want and generates complex, professional MIDI chord progressions.

*   **Key Features:** Natural language prompting (e.g., "Give me a dark, jazz-influenced progression in D minor"), and direct MIDI export.
*   **Best For:** Songwriters and producers who want to explore complex theory without needing a degree in musicology.
*   **Affiliate Potential:** High.

### 3. VIXSOUND (Best for MIDI Generation)
VIXSOUND is a powerhouse for electronic music producers, particularly those using Ableton Live. It can generate drum patterns, basslines, and lead melodies that actually make musical sense.

*   **Key Features:** Deep integration with major DAWs, genre-specific MIDI engines, and a "variations" tool to keep your arrangements interesting.
*   **Best For:** Techno, House, and Hip-Hop producers who want to speed up their arrangement process.
*   **Affiliate Potential:** High.

### 4. Lalal.ai (Best for Stem Separation)
Need to sample a vocal but can't find the acapella? Lalal.ai is the world leader in "source separation." It uses AI to split any song into its component parts: vocals, drums, bass, piano, and more.

*   **Key Features:** Incredible clean separation with minimal artifacts, support for multiple file formats, and a simple batch-upload interface.
*   **Best For:** Remixers, DJs, and producers who love sampling.
*   **Affiliate Potential:** High (Very popular utility tool).

### 5. Synthesizer V (Best for AI Vocals)
Synthesizer V is the closest thing we have to a real human singer in a box. Unlike the robotic voices of the past, Synthesizer V’s voices (like "Solaria") can breathe, express emotion, and hit complex vibratos.

*   **Key Features:** Pitch and tension control, multilingual support, and a dedicated editor that feels like a piano roll for the human voice.
*   **Best For:** Producers who write songs but can't find a vocalist, or for creating high-quality demos.

### 6. Landr (Best for AI Mastering)
Landr was a pioneer in AI mastering, and it remains the best in the business. It analyzes your track against thousands of professional recordings to give you a radio-ready master in seconds.

*   **Key Features:** Adaptive AI engine, curated "styles" (Warm, Balanced, Open), and an integrated distribution platform to get your music on Spotify.
*   **Best For:** Independent artists who want a professional sound without the $100/track price tag of a mastering engineer.
*   **Affiliate Potential:** High.

### 7. Orb Producer Suite (Best for AI-Assisted Arrangement)
Orb is a collection of four plugins (Chords, Melody, Bass, and Synth) that work together to create an entire song structure. It’s designed to be used *inside* your DAW.

*   **Key Features:** Thousands of unique patterns, "Polyrhythms" engine, and a "Smart AI" that ensures all four plugins stay in the same key and scale.
*   **Best For:** Creating the "foundation" of a track that you can then manually tweak and polish.

## Workflow: From AI Idea to DAW Masterpiece

To get the most out of these tools, we recommend the **"AI-First, Human-Last"** approach:

1.  **Generate the Spark:** Use **Suno** to get a feel for a melody or a vibe.
2.  **Extract the MIDI:** Use **ChordGen** to get the actual notes into your DAW.
3.  **Swap the Sounds:** Replace the AI's default sounds with your favorite VSTs (Serum, Kontakt, etc.).
4.  **Add the Human Touch:** Manually adjust the velocities, timing, and arrangement.
5.  **Final Polish:** Run the final mix through **Landr** to get it ready for release.

## Ethics, Copyright, and the Law

The biggest question in AI music today is: **Who owns the copyright?**

*   **Raw AI Output:** In most countries, you cannot copyright a song generated purely by an AI without human intervention.
*   **AI-Assisted Work:** If you use AI to generate MIDI and then perform, arrange, and mix it yourself, you likely own the resulting copyright.
*   **Sample Usage:** Be careful using "voice clones" of famous artists. This is a legal grey area and could lead to your music being taken down from streaming platforms.

### FAQ

**Q: Does AI-generated music sound "soulless"?**
A: Only if you let it. If you use the raw output of a tool without any human editing, it can feel repetitive. But if you use it as a starting point for your own creativity, it’s indistinguishable from "human" music.

**Q: Can I use Suno songs on Spotify?**
A: Yes, if you have a paid subscription, Suno grants you the commercial rights to the songs you generate. However, remember that you may not be able to "copyright" the song in the traditional sense.

**Q: Will AI replace professional mixing engineers?**
A: For top-tier, high-budget records, human engineers are still superior because they can make emotional, non-linear decisions. But for the 90% of independent artists on a budget, AI mastering is a game-changer.
